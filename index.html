The average lifespan of a Worcester Bosch boiler is typically between 10 to 15 years. However, as your boiler approaches this age, you may start experiencing more frequent issues. While repairs can be a temporary fix, it’s important to consider a replacement when recurring problems arise.

If your Worcester Bosch boiler is failing to ignite, several factors could be at play. One common reason is that the thermostat is set too low, preventing the boiler from activating.
